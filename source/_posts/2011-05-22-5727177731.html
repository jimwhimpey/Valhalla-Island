--- layout: post title: "The Truly Silent Phone" ---

I don’t like the concept of phones. It shouldn’t be socially acceptable to interrupt anyone at any time with a demand for their complete attention. That said, situations exist where information is important enough to warrant interruption.

To partly deal with it I rarely had my phone set to anything but silent. In reality “silent” mode with vibration on is actually quite loud and jarring, much more jarring than many ringtones and especially if your phone is sitting on a hard surface. Even in a pocket I find the vibration more of a shock than a vibration-less ringtone.

It’s only recently that I identified anxiety attached my ringing and vibrating phone and the attention it demanded. So I decided that as well as permanent silent mode I’d turn off vibrate and I haven’t looked back, it’s a joy.

If my phone is in view the most notification I get of a call or text is a lit up screen. Much less distracting and more easily ignored than a loud ring or vibrate. If it’s not in view I get no notification at all. Even when in view I get zero notification of new email, an added bonus.

Sure I miss a lot of calls but in a couple of months it’s never mattered, people leave a message and can wait until I next check my phone and I’m free to call. If I’m expecting a call and my phone isn’t within peripheral vision I’ll temporarily turn off silent mode.

It’s a simple change and it may sound dramatic but it feels liberating.
