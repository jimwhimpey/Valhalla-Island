--- layout: post title: "Qantas" ---

I am pro union because I am pro people. Profits to a non-human legal entity at the expense of actual people (or safety, or a quality experience) is crazy and it blows my mind that any person could think another way.

One of the bridesmaids at the wedding reception I was at last night was flying home to the US at 11am the next day. At around 11pm she found out Alan Joyce ordered the grounding of Qantas’ entire fleet. By around 1am, after furious back and forth on the phone at her best friend’s wedding, she’d organised an alternative flight on Virgin that left in just 5 hours.

This is what Joyce has chosen to do to 10s of 1000s of passengers instead of keeping Australia’s airline jobs in Australia and paying workers like pilots and engineers, who are responsible for people’s safety, what they’re worth. Refusing to even negotiate with the unions has already cost Qantas a huge portion of what the unions’ demands would cost, and that’s not to mention the 1000s of customers plans thrown into disarray. Grounding the whole fleet is costing $20m each day while destroying Qantas’ reputation.

Alan Kohler opened with a great question on Inside Business this morning:

Alan Joyce, you said yesterday that the industrial action was killing Qantas slowly, have you decided to kill it quickly instead?

Meanwhile, just two days ago, shareholders inexplicably voted to give Joyce at 70% pay rise to $5m. The absurdity of this situation is mind boggling. There’s a new spirit indeed, a spirit of fucking people over.
