--- layout: post title: "The 'Book' is Dead" --- The "Book" is Dead

Mark Pilgram on David Flanagan having to get a salaried job after 15 years living on book sale royalties and the problem of piracy.

I couldn’t agree more with the comments Pilgrim quotes. So few people have an inherent desire to pirate and it’s an accessible option to even less. iTunes “beat” piracy by being better. iTunes is easier, faster and more reliable, it’s simply a more desirable option even to those with the technical acumen to pirate content. With the “bonus” of being the ethical option.

As commenter Peter points out:

I have been an OReilly Safari subscriber for several years. I can recommend this to every developer out there. … Yet, must admit it still pains me that for ~$500/year we as honest subscribers can not get the same convenience (offline access, unencumbered PDF’s) as people who just download a pirated PDF library for free.

Piracy doesn’t “win” for books because it’s free but because it’s better.
