--- layout: post title: "Power" ---

Car commercials dominate almost the only kind of television I watch, sport. With no exceptions, every car advertised mentions its impressively powerful engine, many running entire campaigns around performance.

I don’t understand it. The Smart Car with its 3 cylinder, 1 litre engine has a top speed of 130 km/h, 20km/h beyond the highest legal speed limit of any road in Australia. How is any power beyond that, always at the expense of fuel economy, practical let alone a selling point?

Why are 6 cylinder cars even something you can buy? Is there anything else for sale in the world so needlessly excessive? Every equivalent I can imagine is totally insane. Like a 60 metre wide screen for your lounge room.
