--- layout: post title: "Minimal Mac's Menu Bar Vendetta" ---

Almost once a day Minimal Mac features a post about how to cut stuff out of your menu bar. The most common removal is the time and date, then replaced by DateLine, a timeline style calendar that sits permanently on and spans the entire desktop. How that scores more minimalism points than the current date a click away in the menu bar, iCal or the dashboard, I don’t know.

Beside that, applications running in the menu bar has got to be the most unobtrusive, ‘minimalist’ option. I’ve seen unwieldy menu bars but the problem isn’t the menu bar, it’s running unused applications. Hiding the dock? Easy. Getting every icon off your desktop? Easy. Minimal Mac’s assault on the menu bar is minimalism for the sake of minimalism at the expense of utility and convenience.
