--- layout: post title: "The Shareable Future of Cities" --- The Shareable Future of Cities

Alex Steffen makes the eloquent case against simply using clean energy to solve our climate problem.

This explains why, even if electric cars were readily available and they could be charged on 100% clean energy I have very little interest in owning one despite not owning a car now partially for environmental reasons. I would much rather (and do) live close to the things I need and want to do. Electric or not, cars are backed up from my house to the city each morning. I pass hundreds of gridlocked cars on my 7 minute bike ride to work, they’re already unsustainable.

He ends with this great quote:

Because right now our economy by and large operates as Paul Hawkins said by stealing the future, selling it in the present and calling it GDP.
