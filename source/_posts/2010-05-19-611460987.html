The Subaru system, called Eyesight, uses two stereo cameras mounted at the top of the windscreen to study the road, cars, bicycles and pedestrians to avoid collisions.
—

Stephen Ottley reporting for the The Age on Subaru’s new pedestrian and cyclist friendly car.

As a road riding cyclist who’s been hit by a car, this cyclist “friendly” car scares me. It does nothing but remove the onus for concentrating on the road from drivers already not paying enough attention.

The system has some nice practical applications though:

It can also detect if the car ahead has taken off at the traffic lights and give the driver a reminder audio warning to start driving.

and:

Eyesight can detect if the driver accidentally selects ‘Drive’ instead of reverse and limit the amount of power delivered to the wheels.

Great, a stopgap for stupidity! No longer is stupidity an obstacle when it comes to controlling a high powered, three ton chuck of steel.
