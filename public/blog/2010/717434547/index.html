New Features in iOS4 →

My iPhone 3G is starting to show its age. The home button is hard to press properly and there’s some scuffs on the screen but mostly the software, especially third party software, feels slow.

Luckily my iPhone 3G contract ends in August so I’ll be able to pick up the iPhone 4 not too long after it’s released without having to pay any more than I do now. Besides a massive speed jump (same processor as the iPad but double the RAM!) iPhone 4 will unlock a few iOS features not available to older iPhones. Here’s the new features I’m most looking forward to:

  • Spell check. Often on the iPhone I’ll find myself guessing letters, deleting and guessing again until the autocomplete kicks in to spell a word I don’t know how to spell. A real spell checker is better.
  • Blue tooth keyboard support. This is awesome. I used to use BluePhoneElite to send text messages through my phone from my computer. Typing out even an average length message on an iPhone sucks when you’ve got a keyboard in front of you. I’m interested to see how this will work with a Bluetooth keyboard already paired with a computer, ideally there’d be a switch to flip pairing.
  • Screen orientation lock. It doesn’t bother me that it’s only a software switch because I’ll turn it on and never turn it off again.
  • All in one Mail inbox. Long overdue.

Judging by this it looks like one of my top three iPhone activities — reading Instapaper articles — is going to be vastly improved. I can’t wait to actually see it.
