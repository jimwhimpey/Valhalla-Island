Bike Snob, as he does so often, strikes to the heart of an issue with casual cycling parlance. Here on a big bicycle tour “holiday” through the desert:

Ideally, we would not need such vacations, instead weaving strands of responsibility and pleasure into the laterally stiff yet vertically compliant crabon frib√© of a balanced life.

This is the way I feel about all holidays. One recurring memory from my childhood is my Mum working her butt off (and getting appropriately stressed) to have everything organised in time for us to take a holiday.

In my year and a half of working full time I’ve taken very little leave and almost none in big blocks. Yet I’ve never felt overworked, stressed or like I need to take a holiday. I work sane hours and lead a rich life outside of work. Everyone is doing it wrong.
